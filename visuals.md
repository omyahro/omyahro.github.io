# Cancer Mortality Disparities Among Black and White Women in Georgia

Cancer mortality disparities remain a persistent public health issue, with Black women consistently experiencing higher mortality rates across multiple cancer types. This data story analyzes cancer mortality patterns among Black and White women in Georgia across two time periods (2016–2020 and 2020–2024). Using publicly available health data, the analysis compares age-adjusted mortality rates and death counts across race, cancer type, and county-level patterns. While overall mortality rates show slight declines over time, disparities remain consistent and, in some areas, pronounced. The findings highlight that Black women continue to face disproportionately higher cancer mortality, particularly in counties such as DeKalb. By combining statistical analysis, machine learning, and interactive dashboards, this project demonstrates how localized, race-specific data reveals inequities that broader trends often hide.
