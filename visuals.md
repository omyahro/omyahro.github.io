# Cancer Mortality Disparities Among Black and White Women in Georgia

Cancer mortality disparities remain a serious public health issue, especially for Black women who continue to experience higher mortality rates across multiple cancer types. This data story examines cancer mortality patterns among Black and White women in Georgia across two time periods: 2016–2020 and 2020–2024. Using publicly available data, the analysis compares mortality rates and death counts across race, cancer type, and county-level patterns. While overall mortality has slightly declined over time, disparities persist. Black women consistently experience higher mortality rates across cancer types, with especially large gaps in counties such as DeKalb. This project combines statistical analysis, machine learning, and interactive visualization to highlight where inequities remain and why localized, race-specific data is critical for public health decision-making.
