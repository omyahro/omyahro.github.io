Capstone Project: Cancer Mortality Disparities

My capstone project examines racial disparities in cancer mortality among Black and White women in Georgia across two time periods (2016–2020 and 2020–2024). Using public health data, I analyzed both mortality rates and death counts across cancer types and counties to identify persistent inequities.

This project integrates data cleaning (Python), statistical analysis, machine learning, and interactive dashboards (Tableau & Flourish) to tell a clear, data-driven story. Findings show that Black women consistently experience higher cancer mortality rates, with especially large disparities in counties like DeKalb.
