Capstone Project: Cancer Mortality Disparities Among Black and White Women in Georgia

This capstone project analyzes racial disparities in cancer mortality among Black and White women in Georgia across two time periods (2016–2020 and 2020–2024). Using public health data, the analysis compares mortality rates and death counts across cancer types and counties to identify where disparities persist.

The project integrates data cleaning (Python), statistical analysis, and machine learning with interactive dashboards built in Tableau and Flourish. Findings show that Black women consistently experience higher cancer mortality rates across cancer types, with especially large disparities in counties such as DeKalb.
