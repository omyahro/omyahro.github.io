Capstone Project: Cancer Mortality Disparities

This capstone project examines racial disparities in cancer mortality among Black and White women in Georgia across two time periods (2016–2020 and 2020–2024). Using public health data, the analysis combines statistical methods, machine learning, and interactive data visualization to identify persistent inequities in cancer outcomes.

The project includes exploratory data analysis, hypothesis testing, predictive modeling, and interactive dashboards built in Tableau and Flourish. Findings show that Black women consistently experience higher cancer mortality rates, with especially large disparities at the county level, highlighting the need for more equitable healthcare access and targeted interventions.
